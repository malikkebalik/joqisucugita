    American journalist, novelist, and Kennedy family member Maria Shriver was born in Chicago, Illinois. NBC News hired Shriver after she worked at CBS. She anchors and correspondents for the Today Show and NBC Nightly News. Shriver co-anchored NBC’s 1988 Summer Olympics coverage and won a Peabody Award. The journalist has strong familial links because her mother Eunice Kennedy Shriver is a Kennedy.

    Parents Eunice Kennedy and Sargent Shriver

    Maria Shriver was born to Eunice Kennedy and Sargent Shriver. Eunice Kennedy Shriver was from the Kennedy family, a prominent American political family. Eunice Kennedy Shriver, born in Brookline, Massachusetts, founded the Special Olympics, a global sports organization for persons with intellectual impairments.

    Her commitment to this cause gained her a global reputation. However, Maria Shriver’s father, Sargent, was born in Westminster, Maryland. He established the Peace Corps under President John F. Kennedy and had a distinguished public service career. Sargent Shriver was the US Ambassador to France and worked in political and social organizations.

    As the daughter of important and talented parents, Maria Shriver inherited public service, activism, and advocacy. Maria’s journalism and activism are shaped by their dedication to improving the globe.

    Maria Shriver Siblings

    Maria Shriver has four brothers: Anthony, Bobby, Mark, and Timothy. Each has excelled in their areas. Anthony Shriver founded Best Buddies International, a charity that helps people with philosophical and developmental impairments make friends. Bobby Shriver, a lawyer, activist, and politician, served on the Santa Monica City Council and supported several social issues.

    Former Maryland House of Delegate Mark Shriver wrote “A Good Man: Rediscovering My Father, Sargent Shriver.” The Special Olympics chairman, Timothy Shriver, supports intellectually disabled people. The Shriver siblings have continued their family’s public service and activism, contributing greatly to society.
